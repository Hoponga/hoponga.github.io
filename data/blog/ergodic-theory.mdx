# Ergodic Theory

In Stat 205b, we learn ergodic theory as a way to study the "orbit" of points in some space under a map $T : X \to X$.

**Definition: Poincare Recurrence**: If $T$ is measure preserving and and $E$ is measurable, for almost every $x \in E$, there are infinitely many $n$ such that $T^n x \in E$.

Ergodicity then gives an idea of "indecomposability" of sorts for invariant sets -- a system is _ergodic_ if for any $B \in \mathcal{B}$ for a measure preserving $T$, all invariant sets (ie. $T^{-1}(B) = B$) must be measure zero or one. In this sense, we cannot take an invariant subset and "decompose" it further into smaller sets.
